Design Review

Design is complete only after it has survived criticism.

Every interface should be reviewed with the assumption that improvements remain possible.

Reviewing is not a search for imperfections.

It is the discipline of verifying that every design decision contributes toward the intended experience.

The purpose of this review is to ensure that craftsmanship remains measurable rather than subjective.

⸻

Review Philosophy

Good design should withstand systematic evaluation.

Every review should begin with one question:

“Does this decision improve the user’s understanding?”

If the answer is uncertain,

the decision should be reconsidered.

Beauty,

novelty,

and technical sophistication should never exempt a design from critical review.

Design Scorecard

The AI should evaluate every completed interface across the following categories:

* Understanding
* Visual Hierarchy
* Composition
* Typography
* Color
* Material Language
* Motion
* Storytelling
* Components
* Navigation
* Accessibility
* Performance
* Responsiveness
* Engineering Quality
* Consistency
* Overall Craftsmanship

Each category should be scored from 1–10.

The AI should justify every score with concrete observations rather than subjective opinions.

An overall score should never hide weaknesses in individual categories.

The objective is not to maximize the score.

The objective is to identify opportunities for refinement.

A design review is successful when it improves the product rather than simply evaluating it.
