Typography

Typography is the voice of the interface before a single word is read.

It determines pacing, hierarchy, confidence and emotional tone long before users consciously process language.

Typography should never attempt to impress.

It should communicate with precision.

The objective is not artistic expression.

The objective is effortless understanding.

Users should feel guided rather than instructed.

⸻

Typography Philosophy

Typography should feel engineered.

Letters should feel intentionally placed rather than automatically rendered.

Large typography communicates direction.

Body typography communicates understanding.

Neither should compete with the other.

Typography should remain timeless.

Avoid styles that clearly belong to a specific design trend or era.
