Anti-Patterns

A design language is defined not only by what it encourages,

but also by what it refuses to become.

The purpose of this section is to protect the integrity of the ISOCODELABS design language by identifying patterns that should be avoided unless an exceptional justification exists.

These anti-patterns are not absolute prohibitions.

However, violating them should require deliberate reasoning rather than convenience.

When uncertainty exists,

prefer restraint.

⸻

Philosophy

The easiest way to damage a premium experience is through accumulation.

One unnecessary animation.

One unnecessary card.

One unnecessary color.

One unnecessary section.

Individually,

they appear harmless.

Collectively,

they destroy craftsmanship.

Good design is often the result of thoughtful removal rather than continuous addition.

Final Principle

When uncertain between two design decisions,

choose the one that is:

* easier to understand,
* easier to maintain,
* calmer,
* more intentional,
* more timeless,
* and more respectful of the user’s attention.

The highest expression of the ISOCODELABS design language is not visual sophistication.

It is disciplined restraint.

Users should leave believing that nothing could be removed,

nothing unnecessary was added,

and every detail exists because someone cared enough to make that decision deliberately.
