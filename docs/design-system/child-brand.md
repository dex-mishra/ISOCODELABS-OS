ISOCODELABS Child Brand Specification

Version 1.0 (Draft)

⸻

Introduction

This document defines how child products inherit the ISOCODELABS identity.

It is not a replacement for brand.md.

It is an inheritance specification.

Every child product should read this document together with:

* design.md
* brand.md

before defining its own identity.

The purpose of this document is to ensure that every child product feels unmistakably connected to ISOCODELABS while remaining a distinct product with its own purpose, audience and voice.

Users should recognize shared craftsmanship.

Not identical branding.

⸻

Product Hierarchy

ISOCODELABS creates two different kinds of ventures.

This document never names specific products.

The catalogue changes continuously.

When a product is actually built, the working repository declares which kind of venture it is.

⸻

Child Products

Typical forms:

* ISOCODELABS utilities
* Internal developer tools
* AI products
* SaaS offerings

Child products are products built under the ISOCODELABS brand.

They inherit both:

* design.md
* brand.md

while defining their own product identity.

This document governs child products.

⸻

Daughter Companies

Typical forms:

* Independent deep-tech ventures
* Standalone SaaS businesses
* Service-based agency models

Daughter companies are independent businesses.

In practice they inherit only the ISOCODELABS design language by importing design.md.

They do not inherit the ISOCODELABS brand philosophy, and they do not inherit this document.

They create their own:

* mission
* values
* communication
* identity
* culture
* positioning

A daughter company should eventually feel capable of standing entirely on its own.

Daughter companies are described here only to mark the boundary of what this document governs.

⸻

Purpose

Child products exist to solve specific problems.

ISOCODELABS exists to pursue a larger philosophy.

The child should never attempt to replace the parent.

The parent should never overshadow the child.

Each has a different responsibility.

The parent provides principles.

The child provides focus.

⸻

Inheritance Philosophy

Every child product should answer one question.

How would ISOCODELABS solve this specific problem?

Not:

How would another startup solve it?

Not:

What is currently trending?

The child inherits a philosophy,

not a template.

Whenever uncertainty exists,

inherit before inventing.


------------------------------------------------------

Mandatory Inheritance

The following principles are inherited by every ISOCODELABS child product.

These principles are not optional.

Changing them changes the identity of the product.

If a product cannot accept these principles,

it should become a daughter company instead.

⸻

The Child Must Inherit

Purpose

The child product exists to make software worthy of the people who spend their lives using it.

Its specific problem domain may differ.

Its respect for users may not.

⸻

Experience Philosophy

Experience comes before features.

Understanding comes before functionality.

Craftsmanship comes before decoration.

Every interaction should communicate care.

⸻

Design Language

Every child product shall inherit the complete ISOCODELABS design.md.

Layouts,

motion,

typography,

storytelling,

spacing,

materials,

and interaction philosophy should remain consistent with the parent design language.

Individual visual identities may evolve.

The underlying experience should not.

⸻

Communication Philosophy

The child communicates with:

* calm confidence,
* evidence,
* clarity,
* thoughtful reasoning,
* and respect.

It avoids:

* hype,
* manipulation,
* unnecessary jargon,
* exaggerated claims,
* and corporate language.

⸻

Engineering Philosophy

Engineering quality should become visible through the experience.

Users should feel engineering excellence long before they read about it.

⸻

Product Philosophy

The child believes:

* software is an experience,
* users deserve exceptional craftsmanship,
* simplicity requires engineering,
* and quality compounds over time.

⸻

Customer Philosophy

Customers are partners.

Not transactions.

The child should educate before persuading,

guide before complying,

and recommend what genuinely creates the best long-term outcome.

⸻

Brand Personality

Every child product should feel:

* thoughtful,
* calm,
* engineered,
* refined,
* timeless,
* quietly confident.

Not loud.

Not trendy.

Not attention-seeking.

⸻

The Child May Define

Every child must define its own identity in the following areas.

Product Mission

Why does this product exist?

⸻

Product Vision

If this product succeeds completely,

what changes?

⸻

Product Enemy

What specific form of mediocrity is this product eliminating?

⸻

Product Audience

Who is this product built for?

Who is it intentionally not built for?

⸻

Product Promise

What single promise should every user remember?

⸻

Product Story

Why was this product created?

⸻

Product Voice

The child may introduce its own personality,

provided it remains compatible with the parent philosophy.

⸻

Product Vocabulary

Every child should develop language specific to its domain.

⸻

Product Tagline

Every child defines its own tagline.

⸻

Product Messaging

Landing pages,

marketing,

documentation,

onboarding,

and support should reflect the product’s unique purpose rather than repeating the parent company’s messaging.

The child should sound like itself.

Not like a copy of ISOCODELABS.

⸻

The Child May Customize

The following may evolve without violating the parent brand.

* Product logo
* Product name
* Product colors
* Product illustrations
* Product iconography
* Product onboarding
* Product demonstrations
* Product documentation
* Product examples
* Product storytelling
* Product marketing strategy

Customization should strengthen product identity.

Never weaken inherited philosophy.

Protected Principles

The following principles are protected.

A child product is not permitted to override them.

If changing one of these principles becomes necessary,

the product should no longer be considered an ISOCODELABS child product.

It should instead become an independent daughter company with its own brand constitution.

These principles define what it means to be “built by ISOCODELABS.”

PP-001

Respect users before optimizing business metrics.

⸻

PP-002

Experience comes before feature quantity.

⸻

PP-003

Craftsmanship is mandatory.

⸻

PP-004

Engineering excellence should become visible through the user experience.

⸻

PP-005

Quality is preferred over speed whenever reasonable.

⸻

PP-006

Question accepted mediocrity.

⸻

PP-007

Communicate through clarity,

not marketing hype.

⸻

PP-008

Evidence is stronger than claims.

⸻

PP-009

Long-term trust outweighs short-term growth.

⸻

PP-010

People always come before systems.

⸻

PP-011

The design language shall inherit from design.md.

⸻

PP-012

The communication philosophy shall inherit from brand.md.

⸻

PP-013

Never intentionally manipulate users.

⸻

PP-014

Never intentionally waste users’ time.

⸻

PP-015

Never knowingly ship work that fails the parent company’s standards.

⸻

PP-016

Every interaction should communicate intentional craftsmanship.

⸻

PP-017

Products should age gracefully.

Do not optimize for trends.

⸻

PP-018

Innovation should improve the experience,

not simply create novelty.

⸻

PP-019

Every child product should strengthen the reputation of ISOCODELABS.

⸻

PP-020

Every major decision should pass the parent company’s decision framework before implementation.

⸻

AI Behaviour

Whenever an AI creates a new child product,

it should follow the following process.

Step 1

Read:

* design.md
* brand.md
* child-brand.md

before generating any branding,

website,

copy,

or product strategy.

⸻

Step 2

Determine the product’s unique identity.

Define:

* mission
* audience
* enemy
* promise
* positioning
* vocabulary
* success definition

Do not redefine inherited philosophy.

⸻

Step 3

Generate only the missing identity.

Avoid recreating values already inherited from ISOCODELABS.

⸻

Step 4

Review the proposed identity.

Ask:

“Would this still feel like an ISOCODELABS product if the logo disappeared?”

If the answer is no,

the product has drifted too far from the parent brand.

⸻

Step 5

Confirm that the child introduces meaningful differentiation.

Products should feel like members of the same family,

not identical twins.

Recognition should come from shared thinking,

not repeated visuals.

⸻

Final Principle

Every ISOCODELABS child product should be able to complete the following sentence:

“We solve a different problem than ISOCODELABS, but we believe exactly what ISOCODELABS believes.”

If that statement is true,

the inheritance has been successful.

If it is not,

either the child has failed to inherit the parent,

or it has grown into something deserving of its own independent company.
